ATTRACTIONS AND HISTORY

The Gold Souk in Dubai is popular for offering a gigantic selection of gold pieces with variety of designs from all around the world. It is home to hundreds of gold shops, some of which have been there for over 4 decades

Hatta is best known for its magnificent mountains, but one of the main landmarks in this little getaway area is actually a historic one. The Hatta Heritage Village is certainly one of the most important historical sights in the UAE.

The Jebel Jais zipline, or Jebel Jais Flight as it is officially known, has been certified by Guinness World Records as the world's longest zip line. It is a quintessential activity if you are an adrenaline junkie looking for a thrilling adventure!

Although the lake is nestled in a desert, this lush oasis is home to over 170 species of exotic animals, flowers and plants. Some of the plants have even been planted in a specific pattern designed to resemble intertwined hearts, just like the lake itself.

The Burj al Arab Hotel or “Tower of the Arabs,” is said to be the world's only 7-star hotel, and is presently the third tallest hotel in the world. One of the most iconic buildings on the Dubai landscape, the unusual shape of the Burj al Arab Hotel is said to mimic the sail of a ship.

Not only is Burj Khalifa the world's tallest building but it has also broken two other impressive records: tallest structure, previously held by the KVLY-TV mast in Blanchard, North Dakota, and tallest free-standing structure, previously held by Toronto's CN Tower.

Dubai is famous for its soaring skyline – and that's not the only feat of engineering to capture the world's imagination. Built from reclaimed land in a series of artificial archipelagos, Palm Jumeirah is shaped like a palm tree when viewed from above.

Dubai Mall is the world's largest destination for shopping, entertainment and leisure, located next to the world's tallest building, the Burj Khalifa.

Ain Dubai (previously named the Dubai Eye or Dubai-I), at Bluewaters Island in the United Arab Emirates, is the world's tallest and largest observation wheel, with a height of 250 m (820 ft) and was announced in February 2013.
